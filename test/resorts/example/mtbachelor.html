The Mountain Current Conditions

Today at Mt. Bachelor

Tuesday, March 12, 2013

Time of Report: 9:00 AM

Standard Operation Weather is not expected to significantly impact lift operations

It looks like we had another warm night with temps bottoming out at 32 degrees. For you that just means softer snow earlier in the day. Morning corduroy is looking really fun. Groomers are reporting that some runs came out better than others due to the strange temperatures and snow consistency, so be sure to adventure around to find the best riding. Forecast is partly cloudy with a high of 43, a typical spring day. So far so good!

Printable Grooming Report

Two full shifts of grooming crews are scheduled most nights.  On the first (PM) shift, runs are groomed between 4pm and 12am.  On the second (AM) shift, runs are groomed between 12am and 8am. Please understand that during snowstorms, runs groomed on either shift may have additional snow accumulation.

At one of the largest resorts in North America, Skiers, Snowboarders and Nordic Skiers will find great conditions, plenty of snow, big terrain, short lift lines and a great value. Minutes from Bend, Redmond, Sunriver and Sisters, Oregon, Mt. Bachelor Ski Resort offers nearly 3700 acres of varied terrain and sits in the high desert of the Central Cascades and is known for dry snow and the best skiing and riding in the West.
