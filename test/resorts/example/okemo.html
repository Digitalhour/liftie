Report updated Jan. 4th at 7:10 am

The Inside Edge

Good morning skiers and riders! The temperatures have warmed up and the snow is falling, time to hit the slopes! 

Early morning base temperature is 26 degrees and the summit temperature is 18 degrees.  The forecast for today shows temperatures staying in the range of 18 degrees to 28 degrees with cloudy skies overhead and snow flurries.  

This snowy Friday morning, 13 lifts will start spinning at 9 am bringing you to the 113 trails of packed powder and powder conditions. The Black Ridge Triple will be spinning only in the morning and then will switch out with the Sachem Quad which will start spinning in the afternoon.  Race training is happening on Wardance, so that will be on hold. To see what’s open check out our interactive map.

The Okemo snowmakers were out last night blasting away on Switchback, Tuckered Out, Lower Mountain Road, Lower Arrow and the Superpipe.  As they continue to make snow today on Tuckered Out, Big Bang will be on hold. 

Hot Dog Hill is ready for riding with up to 7 features! You can expect to see the Black Out Terrain Park opening tomorrow and the Superpipe later next week. Black Out will be on hold as they put the finishing touches on today, along with Nor’Easter for park construction. 

Normal midweek ticket pricing is in effect for today, so you can hit the slopes for $80 for Adults, $71 for Young Adults and Seniors, and $54 for Juniors and Super Seniors. Half day tickets are $59, $50, and $38 respectively.

More fun can be found around Okemo today at the Timber Ripper Mountain Coaster from 12pm  to 5pm or the Nordic Center now with 22 km of beautiful cross country skiing from 9am to 4pm. Don’t forget about Snow Tubing either, open from noon- 5 pm. If you plan to rent skis for this weekend, visit the Clock Tower and Jackson Gore Rental Shops open Fridays until 10 pm!

NASTAR opens today! Compare yourself against your friends, family members and the U.S. Ski Team regardless of when and where they race. Tomorrow Saturday, January 5th wear your finest red gear and take to the slopes for theAHA Red Dress Dash! Registration will take place from 8am to 9am, followed by a presentation by heart disease survivors. Then join us for a ceremonial run down the hill! Discounted tickets will be provided to all participants. For more information and to reserve your spot please contact Kristen Dziedzic at Kristen.Dziedzic@heart.org.

Snow slide into the weekend at Okemo! THINK SNOW!
