Today's Summary

Life is good folks, especially here at Big Sky Resort. With 18 days of snow fall you can't go wrong, conditions are great out here. But all good things come to an end. Looks like tomorrow the sun will pop out to say hello with warmer temperatures then the past few days but don't get too used to it. More snow is expected for the weekend.

Since our lifts closed yesterday we have received 3-4” and 7-8” in the past 48hrs. Today will be cloudy and snow showers during the day with a possible trace to 2”. The expected high will be around 25 degrees and low around 0 degrees. Light to moderate winds will be blowing from the NW. Our mid-mountain base is at 63” and upper mountain is 95”.

Weather Stations

The Lobo Weather Station site is located just below the top of the Swift Current chair at 8900’ and measures temperature, 24 hour snowfall, total snow depth and precipitation. The 24 hour snow sensor is cleared daily at 4:00pm.

The Summit Weather Station measures temperature, wind speed and direction from the summit of Lone Mountain at 11,166 feet.

The Bavaria Weather Station is located near the top of the Dakota lift at 9,600 feet and measures temperature, relative humidity, wind speed and direction, 24 hour snowfall, and total snow depth. The 24 hour snow sensor is cleared daily at 3:00pm, access permitting.

The Challenger Weather Station measures temperature, wind speed, and wind direction at the top of the Challenger Lift located at 9,675 feet.
